<p><strong>The Sub 24 GHz ISM Radio Transceiver Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>FSK</li><li>GFSK</li><li>MSK</li><li>OOK</li><li>ASK</li><li>GMSK</li><li>FM</li><li>LoRa</li><li>OOK</li><li>PSK</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Sub 24 GHz ISM Radio Transceiver market encompasses various modulation types including FSK (Frequency Shift Keying) which encodes data by varying frequency, GFSK (Gaussian FSK) which smooths waveform transitions, and MSK (Minimum Shift Keying) that offers spectral efficiency. OOK (On-Off Keying) and ASK (Amplitude Shift Keying) encode data through presence or absence of signals or amplitude variations. GMSK (Gaussian Minimum Shift Keying) combines advantages of FSK and MSK. FM (Frequency Modulation) varies frequency for analog signals, while LoRa (Long Range) enables long-distance communication using CSS (Chirp Spread Spectrum) and PSK (Phase Shift Keying) encodes data via phase changes, optimizing range and efficiency.</p></p>
