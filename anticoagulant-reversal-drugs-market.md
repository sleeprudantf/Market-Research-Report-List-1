<p><p>The anticoagulant reversal drugs market consists of several key types. Prothrombin Complex Concentrates (PCC) are used to quickly reverse the effects of vitamin K antagonists. Vitamin K itself is a fundamental agent for restoring coagulation. Protamine is employed to counteract the effects of heparin. Tranexamic Acid helps reduce bleeding by inhibiting fibrinolysis. Idarucizumab specifically reverses the anticoagulation effects of dabigatran. Other drugs may include additional agents and emerging therapies tailored for specific anticoagulants and clinical situations.</p></p>
