<p><p>The Shape-Memory Polymer (SMP) market encompasses various types based on activation mechanisms. Temperature-induced SMPs respond to changes in temperature, returning to a predetermined shape when heated. Light-induced SMPs utilize specific wavelengths of light for activation, enabling precise control in applications like medical devices. Electricity-induced SMPs employ electrical stimuli to trigger shape changes, while other mechanisms include pH-responsive and magnetic SMPs, which alter their shape in response to changes in acidity or magnetic fields, expanding their versatility across industries.</p></p>
