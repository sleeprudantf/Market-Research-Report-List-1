<p><p>Optical Subassemblies (OSA) are critical components in optical communication systems, enabling efficient data transmission. The main market types include Transmitter Optical Subassemblies (TOSA), which convert electrical signals into optical signals; Receiver Optical Subassemblies (ROSA), which perform the reverse function; and Bidirectional Optical Subassemblies (BOSA), allowing simultaneous data transmission and reception. Passive Optical Subassemblies (POSA) serve as integral passive components, enhancing signal integrity. Each type plays a vital role in optimizing network performance and reliability across various applications.</p></p>
