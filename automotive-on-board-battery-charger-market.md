<p><p>The Automotive On-Board Battery Charger market is categorized into two primary types: isolated chargers and non-isolated chargers. Isolated chargers utilize a transformer to separate the input and output, enhancing safety and reducing electrical interference, making them ideal for electric vehicles. In contrast, non-isolated chargers do not employ transformers, offering simpler and more compact designs, typically found in lower-power applications. Each type serves distinct needs, influencing performance, cost, and design considerations in the automotive sector.</p></p>
