<p><p>Uncooled infrared sensors can be categorized into two main types: thermal and quantum sensors. Thermal sensors operate by detecting temperature changes in the infrared spectrum, relying on materials that change resistance with heat, making them ideal for a wide range of applications like building inspections and security. Quantum sensors, on the other hand, utilize semiconductor materials to detect specific wavelengths of infrared light, providing higher sensitivity and precision, thus finding use in advanced imaging and specialized scientific applications.</p></p>
