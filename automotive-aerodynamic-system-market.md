<p><p>The Automotive Aerodynamic System market is divided into two main types: active systems and passive systems. Active systems use adjustable components, such as movable wings or dampers, to optimize airflow around the vehicle in real time, improving performance and fuel efficiency. In contrast, passive systems incorporate fixed design elements, like spoilers and air intakes, that enhance aerodynamics without active control. Both systems aim to reduce drag, enhance stability, and improve overall vehicle performance through aerodynamic efficiency.</p></p>
